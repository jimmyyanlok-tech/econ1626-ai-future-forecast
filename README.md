## Project overview

This project is an interactive web essay for ECON1626 Assessment 3. It forecasts how artificial intelligence may reshape Australia’s labour market by 2030, focusing on productivity, task transformation, skills, inequality and policy responses.

The central argument is that AI will not simply replace entire occupations. Instead, it will reorganise tasks inside existing jobs. Workers and firms that can combine human judgement with AI tools are likely to benefit first, while routine white-collar workers, small businesses and workers with limited digital skills may face greater transition pressure.

## Website structure

The website is organised into the following sections:

1. **Thesis** – introduces the main argument.
2. **Economic Channels** – explains how AI affects productivity, tasks, skills and inequality.
3. **Australia’s Labour Market Context** – applies the argument to Australia’s service-based labour market.
4. **Three Scenarios to 2030** – compares baseline, upside and downside labour market pathways.
5. **Policy Playbook** – proposes reskilling, SME support and responsible AI governance.
6. **Methods, Data and Limitations** – explains the scenario-based forecasting approach.
7. **Final Forecast** – summarises the most likely outcome by 2030.
8. **References** – lists sources in RMIT Harvard style.

## Interactive elements

The website includes:

- A navigation bar linking to each major section.
- A visual AI exposure indicator by occupation group.
- A three-scenario table for 2030.
- Interactive policy buttons showing weak, moderate and strong policy outcomes.
