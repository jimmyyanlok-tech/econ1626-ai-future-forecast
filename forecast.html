How AI Will Reshape Australia’s Labour Market by 2030

This interactive forecast explores how artificial intelligence may change productivity, jobs, skills, wages and inequality in Australia by 2030.

Thesis Economic Channels Scenarios Policy Methods

Thesis

By 2030, artificial intelligence will reshape Australia’s labour market less by destroying entire occupations and more by reorganising the tasks inside existing jobs.

```
Core argument: AI will create uneven productivity gains. Workers and firms that can combine human judgement with AI tools will benefit first, while routine white-collar workers, small businesses and workers with limited digital skills may face transition pressure.
```

AI Capabilities → Economic Channels

AI affects the labour market through several economic channels: task automation, productivity growth, changing skill demand, wage inequality and shifts in firm competitiveness.

Three Scenarios to 2030

This forecast compares three possible futures: a baseline scenario, an upside scenario and a downside scenario.

Policy Playbook

Australia’s policy response will shape whether AI becomes a broad productivity tool or a source of deeper inequality.

Methods, Data and Limitations

This project uses scenario-based forecasting, labour market evidence, economic theory and AI-assisted web design to explore possible outcomes.
